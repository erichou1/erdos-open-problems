OPEN This is open, and cannot be resolved with a finite computation.
For $x_1,\ldots,x_n\in [-1,1]$ let\[l_k(x)=\frac{\prod_{i\neq k}(x-x_i)}{\prod_{i\neq k}(x_k-x_i)},\]which are such that $l_k(x_k)=1$ and $l_k(x_i)=0$ for $i\neq k$.

What is the minimal value of\[I(x_1,\ldots,x_n)=\int_{-1}^1 \sum_k \lvert l_k(x)\rvert^2\mathrm{d}x?\]In particular, is it true that\[\min I =2-(1+o(1))\frac{1}{n}?\]
#1131: [Er61,p.67][ESVV94][Er95e][Va99,2.45]
analysis | polynomials

Comment activity that has not yet been incorporated into the remarks
There are claims of partial results and/or a path to a solution in the comments.
Erdős first conjectured this minimum was achieved by taking the $x_i$ to be the roots of the integral of the Legendre polynomial, since Fejer [Fe32] had earlier shown these to be minimisers of\[\max_{x\in [-1,1]}\sum_k \lvert l_k(x)\rvert^2.\]This was disproved by Szabados [Sz66] for every $n>3$.

Erdős, Szabados, Varma, and Vértesi [ESVV94] proved that\[2-O\left(\frac{(\log n)^2}{n}\right)\leq \min I\leq 2-\frac{2}{2n-1}\]where the upper bound is witnessed by the roots of the integral of the Legendre polynomial as above.

  • Final version:I previously posted here an earlier version of my work on Erdős’s $L^2$ extremal problem for Lagrange interpolation (Erdős problem~1131) (See my first comment). I would like to inform readers that I have now uploaded a revised, substantially expanded, and structurally completed version of the paper.

    The revised preprint studies the asymptotic behavior, as $n \to \infty$, of
    [
    I_n(x_1,\dots,x_n)=\int_{-1}^1 \sum_{k=1}^n |l_k(x)|^2 , dx,
    ]
    where $l_k$ are the Lagrange basis polynomials associated with nodes
    $x_1,\dots,x_n\in[-1,1]$.

    The new version is available on Zenodo at: 10.5281/zenodo.18290797

    \medskip
    The main novelty of the revised version is that it completes the conceptual
    foundation of the proof. Two new sections have been added which place
    Erdős’s functional within the classical theory of minimal $L^2$-norm
    interpolation. In particular, the functional $I_n$ is interpreted as the squared
    Hilbert--Schmidt norm of the Lagrange interpolation operator, and classical
    rigidity and uniqueness results of Kilgore and de~Boor--Pinkus are invoked to
    justify the variational framework (Thanks to @ Prof N Somani and @ Prof Thoams Bloom for reference attention). This resolves a structural gap present in the
    earlier version and makes the argument self-contained.

    The revised paper proves unconditionally that any asymptotically minimizing
    sequence of nodes must equidistribute with respect to the arcsine measure on
    $[-1,1]$, fixing the macroscopic structure of minimizers. It also improves the
    previously known lower bound of order
    $O!\bigl((\log n)^2/n\bigr)$ due to Erdős--Szabados--Varma--V'ertesi to the
    optimal order $O(1/n)$.

    Assuming an endpoint universality conjecture for discrete Christoffel functions,
    the revised version derives the first-order asymptotic expansion
    [
    \inf I_n = 2 - \frac{c}{n} + o!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right),
    ]
    with an explicit constant $c>0$ expressed in terms of the Airy kernel, and proves
    asymptotic optimality and rigidity of the Legendre--integral nodes.

    The paper also contains extensive numerical experiments supporting the predicted
    $1/n$ correction, endpoint Airy scaling, and an entropy rigidity hypothesis. The
    numerics strongly suggest that the constant equals $c=2$.

    For internal consistency and clarity checks, the revised manuscript was also
    reviewed using ChatGPT as a technical writing assistant; all mathematical
    arguments, proofs, and conclusions are entirely my own.

    At this stage, I do not plan to submit the paper to a journal. I would very much
    welcome feedback from experts in approximation theory, orthogonal polynomials,
    and interpolation, particularly regarding the endpoint universality conjecture,
    which remains the only open step toward a fully rigorous asymptotic resolution
    of Erdős’s problem.

  • Edit. I previously posted here an earlier version of my work on Erdős’s $L^2$ extremal problem for Lagrange interpolation (Erdős problem 1131). I would like to inform readers that I have now uploaded a revised and substantially expanded version of the paper.

    The revised preprint studies the asymptotic behavior, as $n \to \infty$, of\[
    I_n(x_1,\dots,x_n)=\int_{-1}^1 \sum_{k=1}^n |l_k(x)|^2 \, dx,
    \]where $l_k$ are the Lagrange basis polynomials.

    The current revised version has now appeared on preprints.org and is available at:
    https://www.preprints.org/manuscript/202601.0875

    Main updates and contributions.

    (1) Equidistribution. It is proved that any asymptotically minimizing sequence of nodes must equidistribute with respect to the arcsine measure on $[-1,1]$, fixing the macroscopic structure of minimizers.

    (2) Sharp lower bound. The previously known lower bound of order $O((\log n)^2/n)$ is improved to the optimal order $O(1/n)$.

    (3) Conditional asymptotic expansion. Assuming an endpoint universality conjecture for discrete Christoffel functions, the revised version derives\[
    \inf I_n = 2 - \frac{c}{n} + o\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right),
    \]with an explicit constant $c>0$, and establishes asymptotic optimality of the Legendre--integral nodes.

    (4) Numerical verification. The new version contains extensive numerical experiments supporting the endpoint Airy scaling and indicating that the constant is $c=2$, together with an entropy rigidity hypothesis supported by numerical verification.

    Update. The revised version of the preprint has now been assigned a new ResearchGate DOI:

    DOI: 10.13140/RG.2.2.11709.93925

    The same version is also archived on Zenodo:

    DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18210226

    I would welcome feedback on the revised version, especially concerning the endpoint universality conjecture (Conjecture~5.1), which remains the main open step toward a fully rigorous solution of Erdős’s problem.
