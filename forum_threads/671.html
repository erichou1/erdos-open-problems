OPEN This is open, and cannot be resolved with a finite computation. - $250
Given $a_{i}^n\in [-1,1]$ for all $1\leq i\leq n<\infty$ we define $p_{i}^n$ as the unique polynomial of degree $n-1$ such that $p_{i}^n(a_{i}^n)=1$ and $p_{i}^n(a_{i'}^n)=0$ if $1\leq i'\leq n$ with $i\neq i'$. We similarly define\[\mathcal{L}^nf(x) = \sum_{1\leq i\leq n}f(a_i^n)p_i^n(x),\]the unique polynomial of degree $n-1$ which agrees with $f$ on $a_i^n$ for $1\leq i\leq n$ (that is, the sequence of Lagrange interpolation polynomials).

Is there such a sequence of $a_i^n$ such that for every continuous $f:[-1,1]\to \mathbb{R}$ there exists some $x\in [-1,1]$ where\[\limsup_{n\to \infty} \sum_{1\leq i\leq n}\lvert p_{i}^n(x)\rvert=\infty\]and yet\[\mathcal{L}^nf(x) \to f(x)?\]Is there such a sequence such that\[\limsup_{n\to \infty} \sum_{1\leq i\leq n}\lvert p_{i}^n(x)\rvert=\infty\]for every $x\in [-1,1]$ and yet for every continuous $f:[-1,1]\to \mathbb{R}$ there exists $x\in [-1,1]$ with\[\mathcal{L}^nf(x) \to f(x)?\]

Bernstein [Be31] proved that for any choice of $a_i^n$ there exists $x_0\in [-1,1]$ such that\[\limsup_{n\to \infty} \sum_{1\leq i\leq n}\lvert p_{i}^n(x)\rvert=\infty.\]Erdős and Vértesi [ErVe80] proved that for any choice of $a_i^n$ there exists a continuous $f:[-1,1]\to \mathbb{R}$ such that\[\limsup_{n\to \infty} \lvert \mathcal{L}^nf(x)\rvert=\infty\]for almost all $x\in [-1,1]$.

          Thomas Bloom — 15:48 on 23 Jun 2026

          • It’s a bit hard to gauge exactly, but often I see that its alignment blocks are somewhat broken, or that it likes to over use parentheses in display mode for domains of things which I then move outside of the display mode as suitable. Also, it often uses a lot of weird paragraph breaks that are somewhat jarring to read so I tend to close that where it makes sense to, and I see that it likes to use random terminology that it has come up with that aren’t standard terms in the literature. For example, the model had previously used terms like “fan” and “template” on a different problem solution which I didn’t recognise and, in fact they had more conventional terminology in the literature that I replaced with because I recognised that. Sometimes it helps to add to the above prompt something like “You should check that all terminology used in your work is standard conventional terminology in the surrounding literature”. Another thing I see the model often do is weird flashy lemma titles or doing it uncapitalised and I try to tone that down a bit. It also likes to be really verbose by restating trivial definitions of things or writing things of the form “Let $X$ be $A$, let $Y$ be $B$, let $Z$ be $C$” as opposed to “Let $X,Y,Z$ be $A,B,C$, respectively”, and that I often switch out as well. It also doesn’t really punctuate sentence openers and I have a personal taste for including commas there.

            All in all though, I don’t often have to modify too much, just places where things are really jarring to read that I know no human mathematician would write something like that.
