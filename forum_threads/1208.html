For $d\geq 2$ let $F_d(n)$ be minimal such that every set of $n$ points in $\mathbb{R}^d$ contains a set of $F_d(n)$ points with distinct distances. Estimate $F_d(n)$ for fixed $d$ as $n\to \infty$.
#1208: [Er57b][Er80,p.110]
geometry | distances

There are no solutions, partial or complete, claimed in the comments.
It is known that\[\frac{n^{1/3}}{(\log n)^{1/3}}\ll F_2(n) \ll \frac{n^{1/2}}{(\log n)^{1/4}}.\]The upper bound is demonstrated by the set of integer lattice points in a square grid (which have $\ll n/\sqrt{\log n}$ many distinct distances). The lower bound was proved by Charalambides [Ch13].

Thiele [Th95] proved $F_d(n) \gg n^{\frac{1}{3d-2}}$ for all $d\geq 3$. This was improved to\[F_d(n) \gg_d n^{\frac{1}{3d-3}}(\log n)^{1/3-\frac{2}{3d-3}}\]by Conlon, Fox, Gasarch, Harris, Ulrich, and Zbarsky [CFGHUZ15]. The example of integer lattice points shows that $F_d(n)\ll n^{1/d}$ for $d\geq 2$.

Erdős and Guy [ErGu70] consider the problem of determining the maximal size of such a subset of the $n^{1/d}\times\cdots \times n^{1/d}$ integer grid, showing that it is at least $n^{\frac{2}{3d}-o(1)}$ (and at most $n^{1/d}$). Lefmann and Thiele [LeTh95] have improved the lower bound to $n^{\frac{2}{3d}}$.

The case $d=1$ is the subject of [530], and Komlós, Sulyok, and Szemerédi [KSS75] have proved that $F_1(n)\asymp n^{1/2}$.

See also [1207]. The behaviour of $F_d(n)$ for fixed $n\geq 3$ as $d\to \infty$ is the subject of [1088].

  • A small observation about possible sharp upper-bound examples.

    Let\[
    Q(P)=\left|\{(p,q,s,t)\in P^4:\|p-q\|=\|s-t\|\}\right|
    \]be the distance energy of an \(N\)-point planar set \(P\).

    The Clemen--Fuehrer--Roche-Newton/Li--Postle argument seems to give the following quantitative form. If\[
    Q(P)\leq C N^3 L,\qquad 1\leq L\leq C\log N,
    \]then \(P\) contains a distance-Sidon subset of size\[
    \Omega_C\!\left(N^{1/3}\left(\frac{\log N}{L}\right)^{1/3}\right).
    \]Indeed, in their hypergraph proof the only change is replacing the general Guth--Katz input\[
    \overline{\Delta}_4=O(N^2\log N)
    \]by\[
    \overline{\Delta}_4=O(N^2L),
    \]and the same codegree checks still have polynomial room.

    Thus any construction proving a sharp upper bound \(F_2(N)=O(N^{1/3})\) would have to satisfy\[
    Q(P)\geq cN^3\log N.
    \]So such a construction must essentially saturate the Guth--Katz distance-energy bound. In particular, low-energy or pseudo-random-looking distance colourings cannot be extremal witnesses.

    As a quick application, for rectangular grids \([W]\times[H]\), \(W\geq H\), the standard divisor decomposition gives\[
    Q([W]\times[H])=O(W^3H^3+W^2H^4\log^3 H).
    \]Hence sufficiently elongated rectangles, for example \(W\geq H\log^3H\), already contain distance-Sidon subsets of size\[
    \Omega\!\left(N^{1/3}(\log N)^{1/3}\right),
    \]and cannot be sharp \(O(N^{1/3})\) examples.

  • With the help of GPT-5.5 Pro, I have obtained a polynomial improvement to the upper bound for this problem:\[
    F_2(n) \ll n^{1/2-\varepsilon}
    \]for some absolute constant \(\varepsilon>0\). This improves the previous square-grid upper bound \(F_2(n)\ll n^{1/2-o(1)}\).

    The manuscript is available here: https://github.com/lsngchl/Erdos1208

    The main idea is to combine the number-field towers of bounded root discriminant used in the recent OpenAI unit-distance construction with a split-prime argument of Pohoata and Sheffer.

    I have checked the mathematical details of the proof myself, but I am not an expert in this area, so I would be very grateful for any comments, corrections, or pointers to relevant prior work.

  • The planar lower bound for $F_2(n)$ has recently been improved. Clemen, Führer and Roche-Newton prove that every finite $P\subset\mathbb{R}^2$ contains a subset $S\subset P$ with $|S|\gg|P|^{1/3}$ all of whose pairwise distances are distinct (a "distance Sidon set"), thereby removing the $(\log n)^{1/3}$ factor in the bound of Charalambides [Ch13]. That is,\[ n^{1/3}\ll F_2(n)\ll \frac{n^{1/2}}{(\log n)^{1/4}}. \]The proof combines the Guth–Katz distance-energy estimate with a theorem of Li and Postle on the independence number of hypergraphs satisfying suitable edge-distribution conditions, together with tools from incidence geometry. The improvement is for the plane only: the higher-dimensional bounds ([Th95], [CFGHUZ15]) and the spherical version of the problem are unchanged, and the gap to the upper bound remains open.

    Reference: F. C. Clemen, J. Führer, O. Roche-Newton, "Geometric Sidon Problems", arXiv:2606.05841 (June 2026).
