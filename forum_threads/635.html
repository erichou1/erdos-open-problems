Let $t\geq 1$ and $A\subseteq \{1,\ldots,N\}$ be such that whenever $a,b\in A$ with $b-a\geq t$ we have $b-a\nmid b$. How large can $\lvert A\rvert$ be? Is it true that\[\lvert A\rvert \leq \left(\frac{1}{2}+o_t(1)\right)N?\]
#635: [Gu83][Ru99]
number theory

Comment activity that has not yet been incorporated into the remarks
There are no solutions, partial or complete, claimed in the comments.
Asked by Erdős in a letter to Ruzsa in around 1980. Erdős observes that when $t=1$ the maximum possible is\[\lvert A\rvert=\left\lfloor\frac{N+1}{2}\right\rfloor,\]achieved by taking $A$ to be all odd numbers in $\{1,\ldots,N\}$. He also observes that when $t=2$ there exists such an $A$ with\[\lvert A\rvert \geq \frac{N}{2}+c\log N\]for some constant $c>0$: take $A$ to be the union of all odd numbers together with numbers of the shape $2^k$ with $k$ odd.

The second question has been resolved in the affirmative by ChatGPT-5.2 (prompted by Leeham); Tao has since observed that a positive answer also follows fairly quickly from an inequality due to Elliott [El79].

    • For Proposition 2, you do not need the condition that $2^{k_0} \ge t$.
      Also a construction for $t=2$ is automatically valid for larger $t$ as well, and the $+O_t(1)$ which can be interpretted as $-O_t(1)$ can be removed.
      (In particular, this proposition is slightly weaker than Erdos' remark, but essentially equivalent.)

      The proof for the last case can also be written down in a simpler form:
      noting that $b-a \mid b \Leftrightarrow b-a \mid a$, we notice that if $b-a>1$ is odd, it is not a divisor of $a$.
      (So one can also do it with one case distinction less)

    • Nice! Incidentally, the deduction of Theorem 1 from Lemma 4 actually follows immediately from an inequality of Elliott (see, e.g., Exercise 8 of these lecture notes of mine, or Lemma 4.7 of P. D. T. A. Elliott. Probabilistic number theory. I, volume 239 of Grundlehren der Mathematischen Wissenschaften. Springer-Verlag, New York-Berlin, 1979. Mean-value theorems.).

      Graphs in which one connects $a$ with $b$ when the difference $b-a$ divides $b$ (or $a$) actually play an important role in the modern theory of pair correlations between multiplicative functions, usually after constraining $b-a$ to be prime or almost prime. Recent work includes Matomaki-Radziwil-T., T., Helfgott-Ubis, Helfgott-Radziwill, and Pilatte. Erdos's question basically concerns the largest independent set in such a graph. It is interesting that Erdos had the foresight to anticipate the relevance of this graph. On the other hand, independent sets have not been studied in the above literature, which instead focuses on connectivity and expansion type properties. However, given that the solution can be deduced in short order from Elliott's inequality, which is contemporaneous with the posing of the question, there is a non-negligible possibility that this question may already be addressed in the literature.

      • On closer inspection of the problem I would actually only mark the second part of the problem as solved (and am reverting the classification on the repository). The Elliott-type arguments would give an $o_t(1)$ term that decays slowly - something like $O(1/\log\log N)$ - but given the discussion of Erdos mentioned in the commentary, probably an accuracy more like $O(\log N/N)$ is actually sought, and this is well beyond what the Elliott-style methods here can give.
