Let $0=a_0<a_1<a_2<\cdots$ be the sequence of integers defined by $a_0=0$ and $a_1=1$, and $a_{k+1}$ is the smallest integer $n$ for which the number of solutions to $a_i+a_j \leq n$ (with $0\leq i\leq j\leq k$ and $j\geq 1$) is $<n$.

Is the number of solutions to $a_i+a_j \leq x$ equal to $x+O(x^{1/4+o(1)})$?
#954: [Er77c,p.71]
number theory

This sequence was constructed by Rosen. Note that the number of solutions to $a_i+a_j\leq x$ is always at least $x$ by construction, but Erdős and Rosen could not even prove whether it is at most $(1+o(1))x$.

  • I think $a_0 = 0$ should be added to the formulation, otherwise, we're quickly getting "no":

    Let $F(x):=\#\{(i,j): i\le j,\ a_i+a_j\le x\}$. For each $k$, set
    $f_k(n):=\#\{(i,j): 1\le i\le j\le k,\ a_i+a_j\le n\}$.

    By definition, $a_{k+1}$ is the smallest $n$ with $f_k(n)<n-k$. Hence:

    For $n=a_{k+1}-1$ we do not have $f_k(n)<n-k$, so
    $f_k(a_{k+1}-1)\ge (a_{k+1}-1)-k = a_{k+1}-k-1$.

    But at $n=a_{k+1}$ we do have $f_k(a_{k+1})<a_{k+1}-k$, so (since $f_k$ is integer-valued)
    $f_k(a_{k+1})\le a_{k+1}-k-1$.

    Therefore $f_k(a_{k+1})=a_{k+1}-k-1$.

    Now observe that at $x=a_{k+1}$, no pair involving $a_{k+1}$ can contribute, because
    $a_1+a_{k+1}=1+a_{k+1}>a_{k+1}$. So $F(a_{k+1})=f_k(a_{k+1})$, and thus
    $F(a_{k+1})=a_{k+1}-k-1$.

    Equivalently, $F(a_m)=a_m-m$ for $m\ge 2$.

    In particular, the error $|F(a_m)-a_m|=m$ occurs infinitely often (for every $m$). Also,
    $a_m-m = F(a_m)\le \#\{(i,j):1\le i\le j\le m-1\}=\frac{(m-1)m}{2}$,
    so $a_m\ll m^2$, i.e. $m\gg \sqrt{a_m}$. Hence
    $|F(a_m)-a_m| = m \gg \sqrt{a_m}$,
    so $F(x)=x+O(x^{1/4+o(1)})$ is impossible.

    • Aha, good catch, thanks. It is odd since Erdős explicitly considers the alternative definition in which $a_0=0$ and the condition is that the number of solutions of $a_i+a_j\leq x$ with $0\leq i\leq j\leq k$ (and $j>0$) is $<x$.

      I guess that Rosen described it to Erdős in the latter form, and Erdős noted that these two constructions produce the same sequence, but did not notice that the count of $a_i+a_j\leq x$ in the conclusion is also changed when switching between the two.
