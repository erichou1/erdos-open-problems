For any graph $H$ is there some $c=c(H)>0$ such that every graph $G$ on $n$ vertices that does not contain $H$ as an induced subgraph contains either a complete graph or independent set on $\geq n^c$ vertices?
#61: [ErHa89][Er90][Er93,p.346][Er97f][Va99,3.52]
graph theory

There are no solutions, partial or complete, claimed in the comments.
Conjectured by Erdős and Hajnal [ErHa89], who proved that a complete graph or independent set must exist on\[\geq \exp(c_H\sqrt{\log n})\]many vertices, where $c_H>0$ is some constant. This was improved by Bucić, Nguyen, Scott, and Seymour [BNSS23] to\[\geq \exp(c_H\sqrt{\log n\log\log n}).\]The conjecture has been proved:
  • for $H$ with $\leq 4$ vertices by Erdős and Hajnal [ErHa89].
  • for the 'bull' graph on $5$ vertices (a $P_4$ with a new vertex adjacent to the two middle vertices) by Chudnovsky and Safra [ChSa08].
  • for $C_5$ by Chudnovsky, Scott, Seymour, and Spirkl [CSSS23].
  • for $P_5$ by Nguyen, Scott, and Seymour [NSS26].
Alon, Pach, and Solymosi [APS01] have proved that the family of $H$ for which the conjecture holds is closed under vertex substitution; together with the three final results above this means the conjecture is now known for all $H$ on $5$ vertices.

Nguyen, Scott, and Seymour [NSS24] have proved that if $H$ is a path then all $H$-free graphs on $n$ vertices contain either a complete graph or independent set on\[\geq 2^{(\log n)^{1-o(1)}}\]many vertices.

A detailed account of this problem, including the proof of some special cases, is given in the PhD thesis of Nguyen.

  • Hello, I have been working on some local formulations around the EH conjecture. I am not claiming proof of EH, and I cannot guarantee all findings in the notes are new. In light of the discussion here:

    https://www.erdosproblems.com/forum/thread/AI%20Contributions

    I spent a substantial amount of time sorting through my notes, keeping what I believe to be valuable, and reviewing existing literature for relevance and novelty.

    These notes were developed with substantial AI assistance over many sessions, and Codex helped me formalize some of the finite statements in Lean. If anyone has time, the parts I would most appreciate scrutiny on are Theorem 2.3, Theorems 3.7 and 3.8, Propositions 5.3 and 5.6, and Theorem 6.9

    1. For every \(m>4\), the singleton-cut rows \(R_m=\{0,\delta e_1,\dots,\delta e_m\}\subseteq \operatorname{im}\delta\) have ladder index \(\le 2\), but every exact affine cell in \(R_m\) has size \(\le 2\), so any exact affine-cell cover needs at least \(\lceil(m+1)/2\rceil\) cells.

    2. For every \(k\ge6\), the first-splitter construction with \(S=\{q_{k-2},q_{k-1}\}\) and \(T=\{q_{k-4},q_{k-2},q_{k-1}\}\) satisfies \(G_k(R)\) is \(P_k\)-free iff the bipartite interface \(R\) is \(P_k\)-free.

    3. For a prime graph \(H\) with a mixed ordering, the two-anchor \((\rho,\lambda)\)-split condition can hold at every positive scale while no positive-width contraction is rich, so two-anchor splitting does not synchronize to a transversal copy of \(H\).

    4. The coordinatewise split replacement does synchronize: for fixed \(0<\rho_0<\rho_1\le1\) and \(0<\lambda'<\lambda\le1\), every sufficiently wide coordinatewise \((\rho_0,\lambda)\)-split blockade is \((\rho_1,\lambda')\)-rich.

    5. For rectangle traces \(\mathcal R(M)=\{E(M)\cap(P\times Q):P\subseteq U,\ Q\subseteq V\}\), a finite set \(F\subseteq E(M)\) is shattered by \(\mathcal R(M)\) iff \((V(F),F)\) is a star forest.

  • The thesis of H. T. Nguyen is a really nice account of this conjecture. Here's what it proves:

    - A proof of the conjecture when $H$ is the five-vertex path.
    - A proof of the conjecture in the setting of graphs of bounded VC-dimension.
    - A proof of the conjecture for infinitely many prime graphs $H$
    - A proof of the bound $2^{(\log n)^{1-(o)1}}$ when $H$ is an arbitrary path.
    - A proof of the conjecture for excluding a hole and an antihole, and for excluding an arbitrary induced $(≥ 4)$-subdivision of $H$ and its complement.

  • This conjecture has been proven true for "nearly" all paths, i.e., for every path $H$, all $H$-free graphs with $n$ vertices have cliques or stable sets of size at least $2^{(\log n)^{1−o(1)}}$ by T. Nguyen, A. Scott and P. Seymour in "Induced subgraph density. V. All paths approach Erdos-Hajnal(2024)"

    In conclusion, the paper proves that $P_5$ satisfy this conjecture.
