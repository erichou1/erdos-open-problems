Let\[V'(x)=\#\{\phi(m) : 1\leq m\leq x\}\]and\[V(x)=\#\{\phi(m) \leq x : 1\leq m\}.\]Does $\lim V(x)/V'(x)$ exist? Is it $>1$?
#417: [Er79e][ErGr80][Er98]

  • Define $E(x) = V(x) - V'(x)$ and $M(v) = \min\{m : \phi(m) = v\}$. Then $E(x) = \#\{v \le x : v \in \mathrm{Im}\,\phi,\ M(v) > x\}$, so the problem becomes, how often does a totient value first appear above $x$?
    One family comes from $v = 2(p-1)$ with $p \in (x/3, x/2)$ prime. Since $\phi(4p) = 2(p-1) \le x$ and $4p > x$, these are candidate hidden totients.

    If $\phi(r) = v > x/2$ and $r \le x$, then $r$ must be odd. If $2 \mid r$, write $r = 2^a s$ with $s$ odd; then $\phi(r) = 2^{a-1}\phi(s) \le r/2 \le x/2$, contradiction. Since $p > x/3$ we have $v = 2(p-1) > x/2$ for large $x$, so the lemma applies.
    Excluding odd preimages $r \le x$ of $v = 2(p-1)$ via Brun-type sieve estimates suggests the exceptional set is $o(x/\log x)$, giving $E(x) \gg x/\log x$.

    This does not establish divergence of $V(x)/V'(x)$ since the Maier-Pomerance factor dominates. But $M(v)$ may be the right object for further attack, particularly for $v \equiv 0 \pmod{8}$.

    • I went back into this again after the earlier comment and found a cleaner conditional subfamily where the inverse tree almost completely collapses. The starting point is still $E(x)=\#\{v\leq x: M(v)>x\}$, where $M(v)=\min\{m:\phi(m)=v\}$. The broad family $v=2(p-1)$ seems too loose because semiprime preimages survive in too many ways. But the following conditional family behaves much more rigidly. Take primes $p=6\ell+1$ with $\ell$ also prime, and restrict to $p\in(3x/7,x/2)$. Then $v=2(p-1)=12\ell$.

      We still have $\phi(4p)=12\ell\leq x$, while $4p>x$. From the parity observation in the earlier comment, any preimage $r\leq x$ satisfying $\phi(r)=12\ell$ must be odd. Prime case: $r=q$, $q-1=12\ell$, so $q=12\ell+1=2p-1$. The prime obstruction only survives when $2p-1$ is also prime. Prime powers: $r=q^a$, $a\geq 2$, then $q^{a-1}(q-1)=12\ell$. Since $\gcd(q,q-1)=1$ we need $q^{a-1}\mid 12\ell$. For large odd prime $\ell$ the only cases are $q=3$ giving $\phi(9)=6\neq 12\ell$, or $q=\ell$ giving $\ell=13$, a finite exception irrelevant for large $x$. Semiprimes: $(q_1-1)(q_2-1)=12\ell$, writing $q_i-1=2a_i$ gives $a_1 a_2=3\ell$. For odd prime $\ell>3$ the only splits are $(a_1,a_2)=(1,3\ell)$ giving $r=3p>x$, or $(a_1,a_2)=(3,\ell)$ giving $r=7(2\ell+1)$, which exceeds $x$ since $\ell\in(x/14,x/12)$. If $r$ has three or more distinct odd prime factors then $\nu_2(\phi(r))\geq 3$ but $\nu_2(12\ell)=2$, impossible. So the only obstruction is $2p-1$ prime.

      This does not give an unconditional result since infinitude of primes $p=6\ell+1$ with $\ell$ prime is unproved. Still the $M(v)$ reformulation and $2$-adic pruning seem structurally relevent once $\nu_2(\phi(r))$ is controlled carefully.   • The sequence $V'(x)$ is in the OEIS as A061070. "On the number of distinct values of Euler’s φ-function" (1988) by Maier and Pomerance prove that it is asymptotically $\frac{x}{\log(x)} \exp\{(C+o(1))(\log \log \log x)^2\}$ for an explicit constant $C$. In particular, $V'(x)$ is $o(x)$. Apparently, "On some functions connected with φ(n)" (1929) by Pillai was the first to prove this. Later, "On the normal number of prime factors of p−1 and some related problems concerning Euler’s φ-function" (1935) by Erdős and "On the values of Euler’s φ-function" (1973) and "Distinct values of Euler’s φ-function" (1976) both by Erdős&Hall proved versions closer to the asymptotic expression above. (See also "The distribution of totients" by Kevin Ford.)

    The sequence $V(x)$ is in the OEIS as A070243. "Some remarks on Euler’s φ-function and some related problems" (1945) by Erdős was apparently the first to prove that $V(x)/x$ tends to a limit, which can be evaluated as $\zeta(2)\zeta(3)/\zeta(6)$. (See also "The distribution of values of the Euler function" (1972) by Bateman.)

    In other words, I believe that $V(x)/V'(x)$ tends to infinity and this was proven by [Pillai in 1929 and] Erdős himself in 1935 and 1945. Color me confused.

    • Okay, thanks to the OEIS links, perhaps I can resolve my confusion?

      The OEIS links suggest that $V(x)$ is actually in the OEIS as A264810, not A070243 as I said. The difference is this: A264810 counts the number of different values of $\phi(m)$ that are less than $x$, whereas A070243 counts the number of different values of $m$ such that $\phi(m)\le x$. If different values $m_1$ and $m_2$ have $\phi(m_1)=\phi(m_2)\le x$, then the former counts it only once whereas the latter counts it twice. Naturally, this makes a big difference for the conjecture.

      I believe that A264810 (as linked presently) is indeed the correct interpretation of $V(x)$, and my own comment I'm replying to was incorrect regarding the meaning of $V(x)$ (but hopefully correct otherwise).
