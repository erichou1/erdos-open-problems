Let $h(N)$ be the maximum size of a Sidon set in $\{1,\ldots,N\}$. Is it true that, for every $\epsilon>0$,\[h(N) = N^{1/2}+O_\epsilon(N^\epsilon)?\]

A problem of Erdős and Turán. It may even be true that $h(N)=N^{1/2}+O(1)$, but Erdős remarks this is perhaps too optimistic. Erdős and Turán [ErTu41] proved an upper bound of $N^{1/2}+O(N^{1/4})$, with an alternative proof by Lindström [Li69]. Both proofs in fact give\[h(N) \leq N^{1/2}+N^{1/4}+1.\]Balogh, Füredi, and Roy [BFR21] improved the bound in the error term to $0.998N^{1/4}$. This was further optimised by O'Bryant [OB22]. The current record is\[h(N)\leq N^{1/2}+0.98183N^{1/4}+O(1),\]due to Carter, Hunter, and O'Bryant [CHO25].

Singer [Si38] was the first to show that $h(N)\geq (1-o(1))N^{1/2}$ for all $N$. For a detailed survey of the literature we refer to [OB04].

See also [241] and [840].

This problem is Problem 31 on Green's open problems list.

This is discussed in problem C9 of Guy's collection [Gu04].

  • Let $f(n)$ be the maximum number of edges in a graph on $n$ vertices
    in which no two cycles have the same length. In 1975, Erd\H os
    raised the problem of determining $f(n)$ (see J.A. Bondy and U.S.R.
    Murty [ Graph Theory with Applications (Macmillan, New York,
    1976).], p.247, Problem 11). Let $g(n)$ be the maximum number edges in an $n$-vertex, Hamiltonian graph with no repeated cycle length. Then $f(N) \geq g(N),$ for any integer $M$, there is $N \geq M$ such that $g(N) \geq h(N).$

    also see E. Boros, Y. Caro, Z. F\"uredi and R. Yuster [ Covering non-uniform
    hypergraphs, J. Combin. Theory Ser. B 82(2001), 270-284.]

    also see Chapter 1 In book: Some Problems on Paths and Cycles

    https://www.researchgate.net/publication/392767794_Some_Problems_on_Paths_and_Cycles

  • I have been experimenting with computational approaches to Erdős Problem #30 (Sidon constant bounds) and recently attempted to improve the best known constant. Our current certified candidate gives:

    h(N) ≤ N^{1/2} + 0.98173 N^{1/4} + O(1),

    which slightly improves the previous record (C = 0.98183).

    I put together an open search + certification pipeline, and all code, data, and certificate bundles are available here 👉 https://github.com/rynald0cst0ltziam/Erdos-Sidon-Records-30

    Feedback, independent verification, and discussion are very welcome.
