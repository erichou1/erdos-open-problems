If $p(z)\in\mathbb{C}[z]$ is a monic polynomial of degree $n$ then is the length of the curve $\{ z\in \mathbb{C} : \lvert p(z)\rvert=1\}$ maximised when $p(z)=z^n-1$?
#114: [EHP58,p.142][Er61,p.247][Ha74][Er82e][Er90][Er97f][Va99,2.35]

A problem of Erdős, Herzog, and Piranian [EHP58]. It is also listed as Problem 4.10 in [Ha74], where it is attributed to Erdős.

In [Va99] it is just aked whether the length is at most $2n+O(1)$. This is true, as a consequence of result of Tao [Ta25] below.

Let the maximal length of such a curve be denoted by $f(n)$.
  • The length of the curve when $p(z)=z^n-1$ is $2n+O(1)$, and hence the conjecture implies in particular that $f(n)=2n+O(1)$.
  • Dolzhenko [Do61] proved $f(n) \leq 4\pi n$, but few were aware of this work.
  • Pommerenke [Po61] proved $f(n)\ll n^2$.
  • Borwein [Bo95] proved $f(n)\ll n$ (Borwein was unaware of Dolzhenko's earlier work). The prize of \$250 is reported by Borwein [Bo95].
  • Eremenko and Hayman [ErHa99] proved the full conjecture when $n=2$, and $f(n)\leq 9.173n$ for all $n$.
  • Danchenko [Da07] proved $f(n)\leq 2\pi n$.
  • Fryntov and Nazarov [FrNa09] proved that $z^n-1$ is a local maximiser, and solved this problem asymptotically, proving that\[f(n)\leq 2n+O(n^{7/8}).\]
  • Tao [Ta25] has proved that $p(z)=z^n-1$ is the unique (up to rotation and translation) maximiser for all sufficiently large $n$.
Erdős, Herzog, and Piranian [EHP58] also ask whether the length is at least $2\pi$ if $\{ z: \lvert f(z)\rvert<1\}$ is connected (which $z^n$ shows is the best possible). This was proved by Pommerenke [Po59].

  • I have posted a manuscript concerning the cubic case of the Erdős-Herzog-Piranian lemniscate length problem.

    For a monic cubic polynomial p, the manuscript proves the sharp estimate

    L(p) <= L(z^3 - 1) = 2^(1/3) B(1/6, 1/2).

    The argument uses the extremal reduction of Eremenko and Hayman and reduces the remaining normalized cubic family to a one-dimensional estimate via the Chebyshev identity for T_3 and the Joukowski map.

  • I have a small-degree certificate for this problem that I'd like to record here, with the explicit caveat that it is not an attempt at the full conjecture. It is meant as the finite complement to Tao's sufficiently-large-'n' theorem (arXiv:2512.12455).

    Finite statement. For every monic complex polynomial 'p' of degree 'n' with '1 ≤ n ≤ 14', the one-dimensional Hausdorff measure of '{z : |p(z)| = 1}' is at most that of '{z : |z^n - 1| = 1}'.

    The cases split cleanly. 'n = 1' is the translated unit circle. 'n = 2' is Eremenko and Hayman, "On the length of lemniscates," Michigan Math. J. 46 (1999), 409-415 (arXiv:0805.2295). The block '3 ≤ n ≤ 14' is covered by reproducible Rust + 'inari' IEEE-1788 interval certificates with row-level SHA-256 hashes. The certificate set is archived under version DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20087919; the concept DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19184467 resolves to the latest version. Source is at github.com/MendozaLab/erdos-experiments, release 'v3.1.0'.

    A note on 'n = 13'. An earlier batch produced a row with zero branch-and-bound evaluations: the BB box-construction step returned an empty vector at high reduced dimension, and the verdict-logic code mistook that for a successful closure. The bug was patched at the source. The 'n = 13' row was re-run with the patched binary on 2026-05-07 and closed at level 0 after 197M box evaluations and 53 minutes of wall-clock; that re-run is what appears in the certificate set cited above. Anyone holding a pre-'v3.1.0' archive should treat the 'n = 13' row in it as superseded -- the symptom to look for is 'bb_total_evals = 0' with an empty 'bb_levels' array and a proof-complete verdict. The version DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20087919 is the post-fix release.

    I'm not extracting a threshold from Tao's theorem here, and I'm not claiming anything for 'n ≥ 15'.

  • We have computationally verified the EHP conjecture for all degrees $3 \leq n \leq 12$ using dual independent implementations (Python/mpmath and Rust/inari) with IEEE 1788 certified interval arithmetic and branch-and-bound optimization. Both implementations produce identical certified enclosures across x86_64 and arm64 architectures. The dominance margins increase monotonically from $17.1\%$ ($n=3$) to $71.4\%$ ($n=10$); all non-extremizer boxes are eliminated at level $0$ for $n \geq 5$. We also derive a new closed-form expression for $L(z^n - 1)$ valid for all $n$.

    Combined with Tao's result [Ta25] for sufficiently large $n$, the conjecture is now verified for all $n \leq 12$ and all $n \geq N_0$. The remaining gap is $13 \leq n < N_0$.

    Preprint and certified result JSON files with SHA-256 checksums: doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19229245

  • Pommerenke [Po59] proves: if $\{z:|p(z)|<1\}$ is connected, then $|\{z:|p(z)|=1\}|\ge 2\pi$, with equality for $p(z)=z^n$. I wondered whether, when $\{z:|p(z)|<1\}$ has exactly $k\ge 2$ connected components, there must exist a uniform lower bound $c_k>0$ for $|\{z:|p(z)|=1\}|$. However, this is false: take $p(z)=z^k-R$ with $R>1$. Then $\{z: |z^k-R|<1\}$ has $k$ components, but as $R\to\infty$ the lemniscate $\{z:|z^k-R|=1\}$ shrinks and its total length tends to $0$.

  • Terence Tao has resolved this problem (positively) for sufficiently large $n$. The paper "The maximal length of the Erdős–Herzog–Piranian lemniscate length in high degree" is available on the arxiv, and there is an accompanying blog post.

    He notes that the problem almost has "decidable" status now, but there may be some issues with testing equality of real numbers.
