\noindent\textbf{Problem Statement:}

Let $\alpha>1$ be irrational. Are there infinitely many primes $p$ such that $\lfloor p\alpha\rfloor$ is also prime?

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Remarks:}

Vinogradov \cite{Vi48} proved that the sequence $\{p\alpha\}$ is uniformly distributed for every irrational $\alpha$, and hence there are infinitely many primes $p$ of the shape $p=\lfloor n\alpha\rfloor$ for every irrational $\alpha>1$. Indeed, this occurs if and only if\[\frac{p}{\alpha}\leq n<\frac{p+1}{\alpha},\]which is true if and only if $\{p\alpha^{-1}\}>1-\alpha^{-1}$, which happens infinitely often by the uniform distribution of $\{p\alpha^{-1}\}$.

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{References:}

[Vi48] Vinogradov, I. M., On an estimate of trigonometric sums with prime numbers. Izv. Akad. Nauk SSSR Ser. Mat. (1948), 225--248.
