\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Problem Statement:}

Let $R(3;k)$ be the minimal $n$ such that if the edges of $K_n$ are coloured with $k$ colours then there must exist a monochromatic triangle. Determine\[\lim_{k\to \infty}R(3;k)^{1/k}.\]

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Remarks:}

Erd\H{o}s offers \$100 for showing that this limit is finite. An easy pigeonhole argument shows that\[R(3;k)\leq 2+k(R(3;k-1)-1),\]from which $R(3;k)\leq \lceil e k!\rceil$ immediately follows. The best-known upper bound is\[R(3;k)\leq (e-\tfrac{1}{6}) k!+1,\]due to Xu, Xie, and Chen \cite{XXC02} (improving previous bounds of Wan \cite{Wa97} and Whitehead \cite{Wh73}). See Eliahou \cite{El19} for more on the upper bound. These arise from the same type of inductive relationship and computational bounds for $R(3;k)$ for small $k$. The best-known lower bound (coming from lower bounds for Schur numbers) is\[R(3,k)\geq (380)^{k/5}-O(1),\]due to Ageron, Casteras, Pellerin, Portella, Rimmel, and Tomasik \cite{ACPPRT21} (improving previous bounds of Exoo \cite{Ex94} and Fredricksen and Sweet \cite{FrSw00}). Note that $380^{1/5}\approx 3.2806$.

See also [483].
