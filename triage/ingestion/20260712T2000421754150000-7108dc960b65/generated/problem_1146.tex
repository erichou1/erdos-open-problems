\noindent\textbf{Problem Statement:}

We say that $A\subset \mathbb{N}$ is an essential component if $d_s(A+B)>d_s(B)$ for every $B\subset \mathbb{N}$ with $0<d_s(B)<1$ where $d_s$ is the Schnirelmann density.

Is $B=\{2^m3^n : m,n\geq 0\}$ an essential component?

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Remarks:}

In \cite{Ru99} Ruzsa states "The simplest set with a chance to be an essential component is the collection of numbers in the form $2^m3^n$ and Erd\H{o}s often asked whether it is an essential component or not; I do not even have a plausible guess."

See also [37].
