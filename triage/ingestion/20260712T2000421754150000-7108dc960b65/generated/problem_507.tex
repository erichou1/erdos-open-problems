\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Problem Statement:}

Let $\alpha(n)$ be such that every set of $n$ points in the unit disk contains three points which determine a triangle of area at most $\alpha(n)$. Estimate $\alpha(n)$.

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Remarks:}

Heilbronn's triangle problem. It is trivial that $\alpha(n) \ll 1/n$. Erd\H{o}s observed that $\alpha(n)\gg 1/n^2$. The current best bounds are\[\frac{\log n}{n^2}\ll \alpha(n) \ll \frac{1}{n^{7/6+o(1)}}.\]The lower bound is due to Koml\'{o}s, Pintz, and Szemer\'{e}di \cite{KPS82}. The upper bound is due to Cohen, Pohoata, and Zakharov \cite{CPZ24} (improving on their earlier work \cite{CPZ23} which itself improves an exponent of $8/7$ due to Koml\'{o}s, Pintz, and Szemer\'{e}di \cite{KPS81}).

This problem is Problem 77 on Green's open problems list.
