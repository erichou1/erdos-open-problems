\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Problem Statement:}

Is it true that\[\frac{R(n+1)}{R(n)}\geq 1+c\]for some constant $c>0$, for all large $n$? Is it true that\[R(n+1)-R(n) \gg n^2?\]

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Remarks:}

Burr, Erd\H{o}s, Faudree, and Schelp \cite{BEFS89} proved that\[R(n+1)-R(n) \geq 4n-8\]for all $n\geq 2$. The lower bound of [165] implies that\[R(n+2)-R(n) \gg n^{2-o(1)}.\]

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{References:}

[BEFS89] Burr, S. A. and Erd\H{o}s, P. and Faudree, R. J. and Schelp, R.
H., On the difference between consecutive {R}amsey numbers. Utilitas Math. (1989), 115--118.
