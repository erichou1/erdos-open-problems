\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Problem Statement:}

Let $u_n=p_n/n$, where $p_n$ is the $n$th prime. Does the set of $n$ such that $u_n<u_{n+1}$ have positive density?

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Remarks:}

Erd\H{o}s and Prachar \cite{ErPr61} proved that\[\sum_{p_n<x} \lvert u_{n+1}-u_n\rvert \asymp (\log x)^2,\]and that the set of $n$ such that $u_n>u_{n+1}$ has positive density.

As with many of Erd\H{o}s' questions, by 'positive density' here he most likely meant positive lower density - in other words, there exists a constant $c>0$ such that for all large $x$ the number of such $n\leq x$ is at least $cx$.

Erd\H{o}s also asks whether\[u_n<u_{n+1}<u_{n+2}\]or\[u_n>u_{n+1}>u_{n+2}\]have infinitely many solutions.

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{References:}
