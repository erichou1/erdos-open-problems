\noindent\textbf{Problem Statement:}

Let $f(n)$ be minimal such that there is a tournament (a complete directed graph) on $f(n)$ vertices such that every set of $n$ vertices is dominated by at least one other vertex. Estimate $f(n)$.

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Remarks:}

Sch\"{u}tte asked Erd\H{o}s this in the early 1960s.

It is easy to check that $f(1)=3$ and $f(2)=7$. Erd\H{o}s \cite{Er63c} proved\[2^{n+1}-1 \leq f(n) \ll n^22^n.\]Szekeres and Szekeres \cite{SzSz65} proved that $f(3)=19$ and\[n2^n \ll f(n).\]
